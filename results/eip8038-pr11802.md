# EIP-8038 PR #11802 Gas Impact

Replay of four mainnet DeFi transactions under the merged EIP-8038 PR #11802 gas
values, using the revm harness at TX_GAS_LIMIT_CAP = 16 777 216 (EIP-7825).

## PR #11802 constants (merged)

Costs are derived from empirical benchmarking at a 100 Mgas/s performance target,
independent of any specific block gas limit.

| Constant | Old | New | Notes |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| `WARM_ACCESS` | 100 | 100 | unchanged |
| `COLD_STORAGE_ACCESS` | 2 100 | 3 000 | cold SLOAD and cold SSTORE first access |
| `STORAGE_WRITE` | 2 800 | 10 000 | every SSTORE write (warm or cold), charged on top of `WARM_ACCESS` |
| `STORAGE_CLEAR_REFUND` | 4 800 | 12 480 | refund when original≠0 ∧ current≠0 ∧ new=0 |
| `STORAGE_WRITE` refund | 2 800 | 10 000 | refund when original = new (restoration) |

EIP spec (AMSTERDAM) is used, activating EIP-8037 state gas for new storage slots.

## Column definitions

- **inner_oog**: a subcall within the transaction ran out of gas and took a
  different code path than at baseline. The outer transaction still succeeded.
  Detected by comparing compute-opcode gas to baseline (proxy for execution path).
- **cap_hit**: `gas_used ≥ TX_GAS_LIMIT_CAP (16 777 216)` — the transaction
  saturated the EIP-7825 per-tx gas cap. Distinct from `inner_oog`: a tx can have
  inner subcalls OOG without hitting the cap itself.

## Results

All runs use TX_GAS_LIMIT_CAP = 16 777 216 (EIP-7825) unless noted.

| Transaction | baseline | pr11802 gas | Δ gas | Δ% | inner_oog | cap_hit |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|:---:|:---:|
| Liquidation | 781 399 | 1 122 399 | +341 000 | +43.6% | no | no |
| Simple arb | 194 977 | 285 877 | +90 900 | +46.6% | no | no |
| Intermediate arb | 1 884 138 | 3 073 038 | +1 188 900 | +63.1% | no | no |
| Complex arb (TX_GAS_LIMIT_CAP) | 10 018 653 | 16 384 620 | +6 365 967 | +63.5% | **yes** | no |
| Complex arb (100 M cap) | 10 018 653 | 16 772 401 | +6 753 748 | +67.4% | yes† | no |

† Minor residual path divergence at 100 M cap: compute gas 142 871 vs 147 695 at
baseline (4 824-gas difference), indicating a small deeply-nested subcall still OOGs.
The 100 M run executes 387 781 more gas of work than the TX_GAS_LIMIT_CAP run and
represents nearly the full execution path.

## Key observations

Transactions see +44–67% increases. STORAGE_WRITE (10 000) is the dominant new cost —
the 1,261 SSTOREs in the complex arb account for ~12.6 M gas from writes alone. At
TX_GAS_LIMIT_CAP (16.8 M), this squeezes forwarded gas for inner subcalls, causing path
divergence (+63.5%). At a 100 M cap, nearly the full code path runs and the true cost
increase is +67.4%. No tx hits cap_hit=no in either case.

**Refund model (correctly implemented):**
- `STORAGE_WRITE` refund (restoration: original = new): both the charge
  (`sstore_reset_without_cold_load_cost`) and the refund (`sstore_reset_refund`) are
  set to STORAGE_WRITE = 10 000, so restoration writes net only the warm static (100).
- `STORAGE_CLEAR_REFUND` (12 480): applied when original≠0, current≠0, new=0. This
  partially offsets the STORAGE_WRITE charge for clearing writes.
- All refunds are subject to the existing 20% cap on total gas used (EIP-3529).

| Transaction | hyp. 3× (eip8038_sstore) | PR #11802 | diff |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Liquidation | 1 265 399 (+61.9%) | 1 122 399 (+43.6%) | −143 000 |
| Simple arb | 289 177 (+48.3%) | 285 877 (+46.6%) | −3 300 |

| Complex arb | 15 947 493 (+59.2%) | 16 384 620 (+63.5%) | +437 127 |

PR #11802 is cheaper for liquidation (WARM_ACCESS stays at 100, so cold SLOAD savings
outweigh the STORAGE_WRITE increase for SLOAD-heavy txs) but more expensive for
intermediate and complex (more SSTOREs penalised by STORAGE_WRITE=10 000 vs the
hypothetical uniform cold_sstore=6 300).
